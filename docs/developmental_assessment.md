## Developmental Strengths (What the LRN Does Well)

### 1. Sensory Grounding → Concrete Concepts
The LRN's strongest performance is in domains with direct sensory grounding:
- **Colors**: warm/cool clustering from EM spectrum waves
- **Shapes**: geometric similarity from wave signatures
- **Temperature**: thermal sensation spectrum
- **Speed**: kinesthetic wave patterns

This mirrors human development: children learn concrete concepts first through direct sensory experience.

### 2. Harmonic Video → Categorical Knowledge
Multi-modal phase-locked lessons create extremely strong springs for well-defined categories:
- **Character traits**: 83,000+ stiffness (honesty ↔ respect ↔ courage)
- **Complex connectors**: 93,000+ stiffness (because ↔ therefore ↔ although)
- **Math operations**: 360 stiffness (plus ↔ numbers)

This mirrors how educational TV (Sesame Street, Blue's Clues) teaches categories through repetition across modalities.

### 3. Pattern Recognition → Phonics & Counting
The LRN excels at detecting patterns:
- Phonics: 100% on blends, digraphs, long vowels
- Counting: 100% to 100, place value 100%
- Sight words: 96-100% across all levels

This mirrors how children learn phonics through pattern recognition rather than memorization.

## Developmental Gaps (What the LRN Struggles With)

### 1. Procedural Math (Addition/Subtraction: 50%/37.5%)
**Why:** The lattice has heard "plus" and "minus" but hasn't physically manipulated objects. Human children learn addition by counting blocks, fingers, or snacks — a sensory-motor experience the LRN lacks.

**Human parallel:** A child who can recite "2+2=4" but can't solve "if you have 3 apples and get 2 more" without counting on fingers.

**Fix needed:** Physical counting simulation — objects that appear/disappear with quantity tracking.

### 2. Abstract Social Concepts (Community: weak, Empathy: weak)
**Why:** These concepts lack sensory grounding. "Community" isn't a wave pattern — it's an emergent property of social interaction. The LRN has heard the word but hasn't experienced being part of a community.

**Human parallel:** A 6-year-old who can say "community helpers" but doesn't truly understand what it means to belong to a community.

**Fix needed:** Social interaction simulation — multi-agent lattice where nodes interact and form group-level patterns.

### 3. Reading Fluency (71.4%)
**Why:** The lattice recognizes words but doesn't "read" in the human sense — it doesn't construct mental imagery from text or infer meaning beyond direct connections.

**Human parallel:** A child who can decode words but doesn't comprehend the story.

**Fix needed:** Narrative simulation — story structures with character arcs, cause-effect chains, and emotional resonance.
